## The Core Argument

Athletic departments spend significant money on athlete relocation — most of it invisible, spread across staff time, coordinator salaries, and third-party vendors. Settlyou delivers a better outcome in 24 hours for a fraction of the cost.

---

## What They're Currently Spending

### 1. Relocation Coordinator Salary
- Entry-level relocation coordinator: **$45,000–55,000/year**
- Mid-level athletic department advisor handling relocation: **$55,000–70,000/year**
- Benefits, overhead, PTO add ~30% on top → **$60,000–90,000 total cost**
- And they're only doing relocation part-time — the rest is general advising

### 2. Staff Time (Hidden Cost)
Every international athlete generates dozens of questions:
- "Where should I live?"
- "How do I get a driver's license?"
- "Where can I find halal food?"
- "What's the closest hospital?"
- "How do I set up a bank account?"

Conservative estimate: **5–10 hours of staff time per athlete**
At $30–50/hr blended staff cost: **$150–500 per athlete just in Q&A**

For a program with 30 international athletes: **$4,500–15,000/year in hidden staff time**

### 3. Third-Party Relocation Firms
- Professional relocation services: **$1,500–3,000 per athlete**
- Used by larger programs for top recruits
- For 20 athletes at $2,000 each: **$40,000/year**

### 4. Athlete Performance Impact
- Unsettled athletes underperform in their first semester
- Housing stress = distraction = worse results on the field and in the classroom
- NCAA compliance risk if athlete housing situations are not properly managed
- Hard to put a dollar figure on this — but ADs understand it immediately

## Common Objections

**"We already have someone who handles this."**
> "That person's time is valuable. Right now they're answering the same 20 questions for every athlete. With Settlyou, the guide answers all of it before the athlete even arrives. Your staff focuses on the edge cases and the relationship — not the logistics."

**"Our athletes mostly come from the US."**
> "Even domestic athletes moving across the country benefit — especially for grad transfers or athletes coming from small towns to a major city. The guide covers everything: neighborhoods, gyms, car registration, healthcare, local life. It's not just for international students."

**"Can we try it first?"**
> "Absolutely — here's a full sample guide we built for a college soccer player relocating from Mexico. [share sample link]. This is exactly what your athlete would receive."

**"We need to run this by the AD / VP."**
> "Of course. Would it help if I sent you a one-page summary with the ROI numbers you can share internally? Happy to do that."
